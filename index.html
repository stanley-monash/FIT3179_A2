Happiness score and their factors

what factors contributes to happiness?

Introduction

This infographic is made to visualize the trends and patterns based on the world happiness report from 2015 to 2019, compiled by Kaggle and obtained from the United Nations.

The causation factors that determines the happiness score are GDP per capita, perception of corruption, generosity, life expectancy, social support & freedom.

The following sections will explore each contributing factors in relations to the happiness score to extract a clear relationship between factors and the happiness score.

Happiness relative to GDP per capita

GDP per capita around the world

On the left shows the distribution of GDP per capita worldwide. Across the years we can observe superpowers such as the US, Canada & Saudi Arabia constantly having relatively high GDP per capita.

But how does a high/low GDP per capita relate to happiness?

GDP per capita and happiness

Continents with a higher GDP per capita have a tendency of reaching a higher happiness score. For example Africa has a low GDP and African countries are mostly located bottom left of the bubble chart, indicating low score.

There is a linear directly proportional relationship between GDP and happiness

Happiness relative to external factors

Social support over the years

Life expectancy over the years

Social support for all continent stagnates over the 4 years but there is a clear hierarchy between the countries in terms of social support, where Africa is always the lowest and South America & Europe stays on the top.

The healthy life expectancy stagnates with continents that shares similar life expectancy score. However, we can spot that Europe always has the highest score and Africa having the lowest life expectancy score.

Freedom of choice over the years

Corruption perception over the years

The values of the overall freedom score stagnates, however using the tooltip we are able to see the freedom score, and we observe that Africa has the lowest mean freedom score, and Oceania having the highest scores.

There is a general decreasing trend of corruption perception score for all continents over the years. We can observe that Africa has the lowest score, whilst Oceania has the highest amongst all the other continents.

External factors and happiness

Social Support

There is a linear trend, with a clear hierarchy pattern where Europe having both high social support score and happiness score and Africa having low to mid. However most countries have 0.8 to 1.3 social support score.

We can conclude that social support does indeed correlate with happiness linearly.

Healthy life expectancy

A clear linear relationship where the countries are more distributed a directly proportional relationship. Africa is clearly seen bottom left indicating both low values whereas Europe is clearly seen having the highest of both.

We can conclude that healthy life expectancy does indeed correlate with happiness linearly.

Freedom of choice

By zooming out we see a linear relationship between the axis However the majority of the countries are located between 0.3 to 0.6 freedom score, indicates that the projected linear line's slope is large.

We can conclude that freedom of choice does indeed correlate with happiness linearly.

Corruption perception

Most countries are centre left of the chart, indicating that a low corruption perception score has a medium happiness score. However there exists a linear pattern as happiness score stays constant whilst perception score increases.

We can conclude that perception of corruption does not correlate with happiness.

What to take away from this?

GDP per capita, social support, healthy life expectancy, & freedom of choice correlate to happiness linearly .

Perception of corruption does not affect happiness, and may have a constant relationship with happiness.

African countries has the lowest happiness scores overall but European countries has the highest.
